    Mindsets by AR

    Stakeholder Management

    1. Continue identify and analyze stakeholders and not just at the beginning of the project
    2. Engage stakeholders regularly via varied channels
    3. Use emotional intelligence to assess and respond to stakeholder needs
      1. Issues with one person should be handled with that person only
    4. Document all impacted individuals as stakeholders, even if their involvement is indirect
      1. if someone is affected positively or negatively, they are a stakeholder
    5. Don't dismiss customer requests prematurely. Evaluate carefully
      1. Don't do nothing when someone has asked for something
      2. Customer requests should first be evaluated for their business value and strategic importance

    Change Management

    1. Traditional - Follow the plan and do not allow changes without an apprived change request but in Agile, it must be done through backlog prioritisation and sprint planning
      1. Any change to the plan must be followed by a detailed change management process
      2. Never implement a change without assessing it firs
      3. Only the product owner does prioritisation
    2. Any stakeholder requesting change must submit a change request
      1. Agile handles changes through product owner
    3. In traditional, all changes must be reviewed and assessed.
      1. Scope changes should be assessed for their impact across all subject areas

    Decision making and Problem solving

    1. Never act without a plan
      1. Planning is done once in the project in Traditional
      2. Agile embraces iterative planning with sprint planning which is done before start of each sprint
    2. Consult the project team before making decisions
      1. Do not take decisions alone
    3. Choose actions that best serve project objectives and deliver highest value to the stakeholders
    4. Understand the root of a conflict before resolving it
      1. Don't attempt to resolve a conflict without understanding the main cause
    5. When an issue happens, enter it in to the issue log
      1. Check the risk register for responses to issues
    6. When confused, refer to, subject matter expert (SME), lesson learned register from past projects or Organisational Process Assets (OPAs)
    7. Always investigate and consult before acting, especially if the question asks what the PM should do first or next
      1. Never choose to fix an issue without analysis
    8. Show progress through tangible outputs such as MVP or Prototypes
    9. Resolve issues at your level
      1. Don't ask sponsors for help solving issues in your projects
      2. Escalate only for approvals or authority limits

    Team Leadership and Collaborations

    1. Be a servant Leader
      1. Empower and support your team through listening, positive coaching and encouragement
      2. Allow the team to resolve the problems
      3. Coach, do not command
    2. Act as a integrator, not just a functional lead
    3. The project team is best suited to break down the work
    4. Team should determine the activity time
      1. Don't do any work by yourself - let the team do the work and Project Manager should only facilitate
    5. Understand team motivations to inspire performance
    6. Maintain strong ethical values
    7. Create a safe environment for disagreements
      1. Conflicts can be constructive
    8. Protect team's focus by avoiding task overload
    9. Use peer learning for skill development
    10. Be nice principle
      1. Don't fire anyone
      2. Don't make anyone work overtime
      3. Don't put anyone down
      4. Speak privately with a miscreant before anything else

    Agile Specific Principles

    1. The product owner creates and maintains the backlog
      1. Train if needed, but don't do it yourself
    2. Use co-location to enhance collaboration
      1. Face-toface comms with whiteboards is the most effective
      2. Provide wall space for sticky notes etc.
      3. Use radiators like burndown charts
      4. Use Kanban to limit work in progress
      5. Agile emphasizes iterations, visual workflows and ongoing stakeholder input
    3. Roll out methodology changes gradually using pilot or phased implementation
    4. Agile requires ongoing customer feedback and continuous validation

    Quality and Delivery

    1. Define quality requirements early and check them often
      1. For agile, definition of done should be well defined
    2. Customers should validate deliverables for scope and quality
      1. For agile, Customers check the quality and scope at the sprint review meeting with the MVP
    3. Use inclusive tools like whiteboards rather than complex software when possible
      1. Low-tech/High-touch
    4. Use bottom-up estimating for more accurate results
      1. For agile, use relative estimating techniques like story points and planning poker, focussing on team velocity and capacity rather than detailed task-level estimates

    Risk and Procurement

    1. Identify and document risks early and thoroughly
      1. Don't start the work until you know the risks
      2. Risks are also continuously evolving and getting documented
    2. Document responses for both threats and opportunities
      1. Risks are stored in Risk Register
      2. Risk Management plan tells you how to manage risks but doesn't list down risks
    3. Use mutually beneficial contracts in procurements

    Project Lifecycle and CLosure

    1. Update the lesson learned register throughout the project
    2. All projects should be formally closed, whether completed successfully or terminated early, ensuring all bills are paid and resources released

    Continuous Improvement

    1. Repeat and reinforce the project vision to the team
    2. Clarify what success and failure look like on the project
    3. In agile, use Retrospectives to review and improve methods
    4. Implement feedback loops - apply lessons from one task to the next

    Cost and Schedule Management

    1. Avoid cost and time overruns
      1. If you must choose, fix budget issues before schedule
      2. Agile manages contraints through adjusting scope
    2. Focus on critical path when assessing schedule impacts
      1. Agile manages schedule through sprint commitment and velocity tracking. Highest priority items are completed first

    Exam strategy & decision guidelines

    1. Watch for answer choices with absolute terms like "Always" or "All", they're often incorrect
    2. Never do nothing
    3. The perfect answer isn't always listed. Choose from choices available
    4. Sometimes there is no correct answer. It is not about selecting the correct one - it is about selecting the best one
